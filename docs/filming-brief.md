# Dillish Filming Brief

One short clip per move, filmed once, reused everywhere: each clip plays in every workout that includes the move, so nothing on this list needs to be filmed twice.

How to film each clip:

- Perform the move continuously for at least the listed clip length, with clean form at a steady, followable pace.
- Face the camera (or the most readable angle for the move) in a consistent setting and outfit style across the shoot.
- A brief spoken form cue at the start is welcome; the app also shows the written cues below during the exercise.
- Where a modification is listed, showing it briefly near the end of the clip helps beginners follow along.

### Russian Twist

- [ ] Filmed
- Equipment: none (bodyweight)
- Clip length: at least 55 seconds of continuous movement
- Cues shown in the app:
  - Lie down on the floor placing your feet either under something that will not move or by having a partner hold them. Your legs should be bent at the knees.
  - Elevate your upper body so that it creates an imaginary V-shape with your thighs. Your arms should be fully extended in front of you perpendicular to your torso and with the hands clasped. This is the starting position.
  - Twist your torso to the right side until your arms are parallel with the floor while breathing out.
  - Hold the contraction for a second and move back to the starting position while breathing out. Now move to the opposite side performing the same techniques you applied to the right side.
- Modification to show: Move within a comfortable range, shorten the set, and rest whenever you need to.
- Plays in: Core Define (2 x 55s)
